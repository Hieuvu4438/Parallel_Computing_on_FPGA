\begin{abstract}
This paper presents an \textit{Uncertainty-Aware Heterogeneous Cascaded Inference} (UA-HCI) framework for edge-based respiratory sound analysis. The framework combines three Random Forest early-exit layers on the ARM Processing System with a MobileNetV2 student network deployed as an INT8 Quantization-Aware Training (QAT) model on the Xilinx Deep Learning Processor Unit (DPU). Lightweight layers screen confident samples using statistical acoustic descriptors, while uncertain samples are converted into Hybrid Spectrogram tensors and routed to the CNN stage. The method is evaluated on a merged ICBHI 2017 and King Abdullah University Hospital respiratory sound dataset for three-class classification of COPD, Healthy, and Non-COPD recordings. Under subject-independent cross-validation, the proposed framework achieves a best-fold accuracy of 96.56\% and a mean accuracy of 94.94\%. Hardware profiling separates Layer-4 DPU inference latency from full cascade latency and defines the measurements required to quantify route-level energy savings. The results support uncertainty-aware FPGA deployment as a practical direction for reducing unnecessary deep inference in resource-constrained respiratory sound analysis.
\end{abstract}
