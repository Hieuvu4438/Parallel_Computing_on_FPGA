\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}
This study has designed and developed a hierarchical embedded system using respiratory sound features to diagnose respiratory diseases. The system was successfully deployed on the FPGA Ultra96V2, achieving the expected accuracy and performance. With a peak accuracy of 98.81\% for five classes—healthy, COPD, URTI, bronchitis, and pneumonia—the model demonstrates the effectiveness and reliability of the proposed method in classifying respiratory conditions. The system exhibits superior parallel processing capabilities and flexibility, significantly reducing disease classification time. This advantage translates into faster processing speeds and reduced waiting times during the diagnostic process. Additionally, the system was engineered to save 52.5\% of energy consumption compared to traditional methods, facilitating deployment on mobile or embedded devices and allowing for use in clinical and real-world settings.

The results of this research underscore the efficacy and broad application potential of the proposed method in diagnosing chronic obstructive pulmonary disease and other respiratory conditions. The hierarchical embedded system, designed with high accuracy, performance, and energy efficiency, paves the way for practical usage in supporting clinical diagnostic procedures and enhancing patient care quality.
